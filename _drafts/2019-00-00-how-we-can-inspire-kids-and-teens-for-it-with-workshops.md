# Introduction
One of the best things you can do in your job career is to share your experiences and knowledge with other people.
Whether you introduce a new colleague to his future tasks, being a mentor or trainer, writing articles and giving talks about work related topics or leading a whole workshop for your colleagues - there are numerous possibilities to both educate and fascinate other people with your work.

A very special way to do this is by inspiring kids and teens to follow in your footsteps.
This is what I recently do a lot in my free time by attending workshops for kids and teens as a mentor.

In this article I want to write about my own experiences and why you absolutely should do the same thing :)!

# IT workshops for kids and teens
## How many workshops did I attend so far and how did I learn about them?
It all started when Oliver Milke asked me on a [HackTalk](https://hacktalk.de/) event whether I want to participate in the next installment of a kids workshop called Devoxx4Kids in November 2017.
So far I have been on more than ten IT workshop events for kids and teens as a volunteer plus some other events for kids who are interested in STEM.
The previous formats were namely Devoxx4Kids, Kids4IT, KIDScraft, JavaLand4Kids, CoderDojo, CyberMentor, MINT for Girls and Girl's Day.

## What does a workshop look like?
There are a lot of different workshop formats with different sizes and goals.
In this article I will only introduce German workshop formats I know about.
You can participate at any of these as a mentor yourself.

### Devoxx4Kids
Devoxx4Kids takes place several times a year in different towns in Germany.
It's a one day event with about 50 attendees.
Kids aged 8 to 13 and teens aged 14 to 16 are allowed to participate in the workshops.
They don't need any previous knowledge about programming.

The topics are diverse and different depending on age.
They include programming of small applications in different programming languages for teens and some smaller topics for kids.
Because it's a one day event the smaller kids are attending their workshops in rotating groups so they get introduced to more than one topic.

You can find more information on the [homepage of Devoxx4Kids](http://www.devoxx4kids.org/).

### Kids4IT
Kids4IT is like a smaller version of Devoxx4Kids.
Just like Devoxx4Kids it occurs several times a year - only with a smaller range of locations (HH, H, BS, WOB and a few others) and age group (8-13).
Kids aged 8 to 13 are learning one smaller topic within four hours.

You can find more information on the [homepage of Kids4IT](https://www.kids4it.de/).

### KIDScraft
In contrary to Kids4IT one could call KIDScraft the bigger version of Devoxx4Kids.
KIDScraft is a one week event and takes place in Wolfsburg during the summer holidays.
Altogether 150 kids (8 to 13) and teens (14 to 16) are learning different topics of computer science on this camp.

Teens and kids are divided in two so called "camps".
The older ones are working on a single large project theme the whole week long.
The younger ones on the other hand get introduced to different topics on the first three days and then have to decide on which of the topics they want to spend more time on.
On friday all children finish their projects and present them to their parents and other kids.

Because of its size and duration KIDScraft is a very special event.
All attendees and mentors are together almost the whole day.
Plus there are a lot of extra activities like sports and DIY workshops the kids are able to attend during the event.
The organizers - [Junge Tüftler gGmbH](https://junge-tueftler.de/) - are very skilled at designing educationally valuable workshops for kids and teens.
I learned a lot working with them! :)

You can find more information on the [homepage of KIDScraft](https://www.kidscraft-camp.de/).

### Jugend hackt
Jugend hackt is a multi-day event, just like KIDScraft.
The events are taking place in different German cities for three days each (friday to sunday).
Up to 120 teens between 12 and 18 are working in groups on a project with the aim to improve the world in some way.
To implement their ideas the teens are free to choose on how to do it.
Some of them are programming an app, others are building a prototype with materials like wood and LED lights.

You can find more information on the [homepage of Jugend hackt](https://jugendhackt.org/).

### CoderDojo
CoderDojo is a worldwide known workshop format and takes place in several towns in Germany.
This one-day event occurs every month on a Saturday.
These are smaller workshops without a set workshop topic.
The attendees - kids at the age from 6 to 16 - are working on their own projects which only have to be related to technology in some way.
The kids are free to form groups or working alone on their ideas

In mehreren Städten in Deutschland gibt es regelmäßig stattfindende CoderDojo-Workshops.
Hierbei handelt es sich um eher kleinere Workshops, in denen die Teilnehmer eigenverantwortlich an ihren Projekten arbeiten.
Die Mentoren stehen ihnen hierbei stets zur Seite und beantworten ihre Fragen und geben Anregungen für neue Ideen.

Das Besondere an dem Format CoderDojo ist die Regelmäßigkeit.
Beispielsweise monatlich finden etwa vierstündige Treffen statt, bei denen oft dieselben Kinder und Jugendliche teilnehmen und an ihren Projekten laufend weiterarbeiten.
Beim CoderDojo können Kinder und Jugendliche im Alter zwischen 8 und 16 teilnehmen.

Mehr Infos kann man auf der [Homepage](https://coderdojo.com/) vom CoderDojo finden.

### Other formats
Neben den etwas ausführlicher beschriebenen Workshop-Formaten gibt es natürlich noch weitere interessante Formate, bei denen man als Mentor oder Mentee mitmachen kann.

CyberMentor ist beispielsweise ein sehr interessantes Format, bei dem ein (weiblicher) Mentor aus der IT-Branche für ein ganzes Jahr eine sichj für MINT interessierende Schülerin online begleiten kann und ihr im Thema MINT bei Fragen und Anregungen zur Seite steht.

Und das JavaLand4Kids ist eine spezielle Ausgabe vom Devoxx4Kids, das einen Tag vor der JavaLand-Konferenz im Phantasialand stattfindet.

Sicher gibt es noch weitere interessante Workshop-Formate.
Gerne kannst du mir schreiben, welche du noch innerhalb Deutschlands kennst, bei denen man als ehrenamtlicher Mentor mitmachen kann!
